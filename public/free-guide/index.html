What's inside

  • 1
    "Tell me about your best friend."Children's friendships at 3–5 are intensely specific and completely forgotten by 10.
  • 2
    "What happens when you dream?"A 4-year-old's theory of dreams is unlike anything you'll hear again.
  • 3
    "What do you want to be when you grow up — and why?"The 'why' is the golden part. Record this every 6 months.
  • 4
    "Tell me the funniest thing you know."Their delivery, timing, the things they find funny — all unrepeatable.
  • 5
    "What do you think happens when people get very old?"These answers change dramatically as they grow. Capture the before.
  • 6
    "What's the best thing about our family?"You will cry. That's okay.
  • 7
    "Sing me your favourite song right now."The 3-year-old version of a song is gone by 5. Time-sensitive.
  • 8
    "Tell me something you know that most people don't."What they think is secret tells you everything about what they're thinking.
  • 9
    "What were you like when you were a baby?"Kids have strong opinions about who they were before they can remember.
  • 10
    "What do you want me to remember about you?"Save this one for last. Some children say something that stays with you forever.

Why this matters

Your child's voice at 3 sounds nothing like it will at 5. By 7, childhood amnesia means they won't remember most of what's happening right now.

A photo captures what they looked like. A voice recording captures who they were — the mispronounced words, the wild theories, the way they said your name.

Most parents know this. Almost none have recordings.

This guide takes 5 minutes. Press record tonight.
